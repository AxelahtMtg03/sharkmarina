The tiger shark's journey was tough. He made the water holes and rivers in the Gulf. Other animals were angry and did not want him to live with them. A wallaby threw rocks at him when the shark asked to stay with the wallaby. The shark helped create the Gulf of Carpentaria.

The myth says the tiger shark created the Gulf of Carpentaria and its rivers. The myths have been passed down by oral tradition through generations of the Aboriginal Yanyuwa people, who call themselves 'people of the sea'. The Yanyuwa people believe the Gulf of Carpentaria was created by the tiger shark.

Tiger sharks are important to Aboriginal culture. Some people believe the tiger shark is their ancestor. The Yanyuwa language has many words for sea and shark.

The tiger shark travels thousands of kilometers from these coastal waters to the Pacific Ocean each year. However, conservationists are concerned about tiger shark numbers. They are currently listed as 'Near Threatened'. Aboriginal people have noticed a decline in the number of sharks, particularly as a result of climate change. Warmer waters make it harder for them to grow and swim far. People still respect sharks as givers of life and creators of this land.

The people teach the Yanyuwa language and about sea animals. The aboriginal rangers guarding the area also look after the islands' ceremonial sites, burial grounds and rock art, which still shows images of sharks.

The Yanyuwa language sounds like the sea, showing Aboriginal people's connection to nature. Losing a language is an important issue. Only three women speak the women's dialect. Yanyuwa is unusual because men and women speak different languages. Aboriginal people were forced to speak English.

Women understand men's language but don't speak it, and vice versa for men. Women and men have different relationships with the shark. Women use a more caring word, while men use a synonym for creator, ancestor. Nobody knows why these different dialects started. Maybe men and women had different roles and didn't spend much time together tens of thousands of years ago. Maybe it was a sign of respect not to speak another's dialect. Nowday, if you don't speak the right language, you can be punished. Men and women don't speak each other's language because it's disrespectful.
