The Global Fin Print project has facilitated the undertaking of research into marine biodiversity across the globe, with a particular focus on sharks, through the utilisation of baited remote underwater video systems (BRUVS) surveys. This will facilitate the construction of databases, given that the decline in global shark populations is largely undocumented. The data collected will serve as a baseline for future studies and the formulation of conservation policies.

With regard to Australia, it is notable that this country is one of only a few (along with the Bahamas, the Federated States of Micronesia, French Polynesia, the Maldives and the United States) where the reef shark population remains largely intact. This is largely due to the implementation of a species conservation and fisheries management policy, which has resulted in limited catches of sharks.

The absence of sharks was identified on only 20% of coral reefs in almost 58 different countries. The study concludes with an alarming report on the global situation of sharks, indicating that conservation efforts are still required.
