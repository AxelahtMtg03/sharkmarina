THE SHARKS DECLINE IN AUSTRALIA

Why is the shark decline a major issue that concerns the whole of Australia?

How can I describe in a few words what has been described as a killing machine, a bloodthirsty killer? A difficult task... The word shark covers a large group of cartilaginous fish, comprising more than 460 different species, including rays. This group of animals is more than 400 million years old (appearance of the xenacanthus) and includes sharks from 15 cm to 15 meters long, living at depths of between 1000 and 4000 meters and distributed throughout the world's oceans. Some live for 10 years, while others, such as the Greenland shark, can live for centuries. Sadly, very few sharks live that long, most are caught, legally or illegally, or die from poisoning or drowning in drift nets. Let's take a look at Australia's shark population. The island continent was settled and developed (from a Western point of view) long after the rest of the world. You could say that the population has been relatively unscathed. However it is not true. The population decreased in 50 years.
